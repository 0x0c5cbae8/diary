Dear diary,

Tuesday, May 19th, 2026. Another one for the books, or rather, another day in the perpetual Caltech sprint. Woke up feeling like my brain had already run a marathon. The *bustle* outside my room in Blacker was already in full swing – doors slamming, muffled conversations, the faint clatter from the dining hall. It’s a sound I’ve grown accustomed to, a background hum that signifies the relentless march of academia here.

My thermodynamics lecture this morning was a blur of equations and concepts I swear I understood yesterday but today felt entirely new. Then it was straight to the lab for my chem final project. That’s where the *bustle* really hits its peak. Imagine twenty freshmen, all slightly sleep-deprived, operating delicate instruments, trying to synthesize complex compounds, with TAs weaving through, offering cryptic hints and occasional exasperated sighs. The air practically crackles with focused energy, the hurried whispers of "Did you get this value?" and the quiet hum of vacuum pumps. It’s chaotic, but there’s a strange beauty to the collective effort, everyone pushing towards a common goal (a passing grade, mostly).

Lunch was a quick grab-and-go from Chandler, trying to avoid the main rush. I found a spot by the olive trees, just for a moment of quiet away from the continuous flow of people. Even then, the campus still *bustled* around me – grad students on bikes, profs deep in discussion, tour groups gawking at the Athenaeum. It makes me wonder if Caltech ever truly slows down.

The afternoon was a deep dive into problem set 10 for quantum, followed by a quick meeting for SURF preparations. The internal *bustle* in my own head is probably the most intense – equations, deadlines, the constant gnawing feeling that I'm missing something crucial. Finals are looming, and it feels like the whole campus has collectively decided to amp up the intensity. Every study lounge, every corner of Millikan Library, is packed with students hunched over laptops and textbooks, a silent, focused *bustle* of minds at work.

Sometimes I wish for a moment where everything just… pauses. But then I remember why I’m here. This *bustle*? It’s the sound of discovery, of innovation, of a thousand brilliant minds all trying to figure out how the universe works. And honestly, despite the exhaustion, there's nowhere else I'd rather be.

Just gotta keep moving.

-Trevor
