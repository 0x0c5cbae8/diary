Dear diary,

Another Tuesday in the books, and honestly, my brain feels like it’s been put through a centrifuge. The whole campus just has this incredible *bustle* to it lately, more than usual. Every time I walk from Fleming to North, it’s a blur of people power-walking with backpacks straining, frantic whispers about problem sets, and the distant thrum of machinery from some lab or another. It’s like the air itself is charged with kinetic energy, and I’m just trying to keep from getting knocked over by the sheer momentum of it all.

Today was particularly wild. Woke up to the usual cacophony of Page House – someone was definitely testing a new sound system, or perhaps attempting to synthesize a black hole in the common room. Then it was straight to PChem, where Dr. Chen decided to introduce quantum mechanics in an hour. My notes are a testament to my futile attempts to capture every fleeting concept; they look more like a spider's web than legible information. Afterward, the library was packed. Even the *quiet* of the library had a kind of underlying bustle, if you know what I mean. Everyone hunched over books, the soft rustle of pages, the click of keyboards – a silent, focused intensity that feels almost more overwhelming than actual noise.

My own mind is a whirlwind. I’m trying to prep for that QED final that’s looming, finish the optics project, and somewhere in between, keep my protein folding simulation from collapsing. And then there’s *the thing*. Everyone’s talking about *the thing*. The hushed conversations, the late-night disappearances of upperclassmen, the sudden need for obscure materials… the *bustle* around it is palpable. It’s a mix of excitement and terror, knowing that whatever it is, it’s going to be epic, and probably exhaust me even more. I just hope my stack holds up. Wait, did I say stack? Uh, I mean, I hope *I* hold up.

I’m already craving summer break, but I know this unique Caltech brand of spring-term *bustle* is part of the experience. Still, I wouldn't mind a few hours where my brain isn't trying to compute the optimal trajectory for a rogue photon while simultaneously remembering that I need to do laundry.

Deep breaths. Only a few more weeks until I can just… not bustle.

-Trevor
