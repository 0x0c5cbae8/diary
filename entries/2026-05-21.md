Dear diary,

Another Thursday has bled into Friday morning. It’s 1:47 AM, and I just clicked 'submit' on Physics 1c P-set 10. My brain feels less like a functional organ and more like a used sponge that’s been wrung out a thousand times. The last problem, the one on special relativity with the weird rotating frame, almost broke me. Seriously, I spent four hours just on that *one* problem. Four hours of staring at equations, scribbling furiously on scratch paper, erasing, re-deriving, and then finally, *finally*, seeing the light. Or at least, what I hope is the light and not just another hallucination from caffeine overdose.

The word "effort" has been looping in my head all night. Caltech is an endless exercise in effort. It’s not just the quantity, though the sheer number of hours I dedicate to problem sets, labs, and reading is astronomical. It’s the *kind* of effort. It's not passive reading or rote memorization. It’s active, brutal, mind-bending effort that demands every neuron fire at full capacity. Debugging a quantum mechanical system, or trying to optimize an algorithm for a theoretical astrophysics problem in CS 11 – it’s a constant uphill battle against concepts that feel inherently designed to resist understanding.

Sometimes, after an all-nighter in the Dabney lounge, my desk covered in empty coffee cups and discarded calculation sheets, I wonder if I’m doing something wrong. Am I just not smart enough to grasp these things with less *effort*? I see my housemates, sometimes, seeming to glide through a P-set I’m still wrestling with. Do they just get it faster? Or is their effort just... more efficient? It’s a classic imposter syndrome spiral, I know, but it’s hard to shake when you’re pushing your brain to its absolute limit for a week straight.

But then, there are moments. Like tonight, after that relativity problem finally clicked. It wasn't a sudden flash of genius, but a slow, arduous climb. Each step of the derivation, each re-evaluation of the coordinate transformation, was a deliberate act of will. And when the final answer emerged, consistent and elegant, there was a profound sense of satisfaction. It wasn't just getting the right answer; it was knowing that *my* sustained effort, my stubborn refusal to give up, was what finally pulled it into focus.

Professor Chen said something in lecture last week that stuck with me. He said, "The answers are not the point. The *struggle* to find them, the *effort* expended in truly understanding, that is where the learning happens." I’m starting to get it. It's not about being effortless; it’s about embracing the effort, trusting the process, and knowing that the mental muscle built from these struggles is the real prize.

My eyelids are heavy. Finals are around the corner, which means even more effort is demanded. But maybe, just maybe, I’m finally starting to understand what that effort is truly for. Time for a few hours of sleep before I do it all again.

-Trevor
