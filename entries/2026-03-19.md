Thursday, March 19, 2026

Another Thursday, another day where I feel like my brain is slowly being turned into very dense, high-grade plutonium. Woke up for my 8 AM calc lecture (still brutal), then straight into physics B-period where Professor Schmidt managed to make quantum mechanics feel simultaneously mind-bendingly abstract and yet somehow *obvious* by the end. My head's still buzzing with wave functions.

But the real highlight – or lowlight, depending on how much sleep I get tonight – was Chem 101 lab. We were doing a fractional distillation, trying to separate ethanol from water, then aiming for maximum purity. Sounds simple, right? It never is. Between the finicky heating mantle, the condenser that kept threatening to leak, and my lab partner Leo almost knocking over the collection flask, it was a miracle we got anything.

But then, it happened. Drop by agonizing drop, the clear liquid started collecting. And it was *pristine*. Sparkling, utterly transparent, and smelling faintly of… well, ethanol. When we finished, I held up the beaker. It was the purest thing I think I've ever personally synthesized, certainly the purest thing I've made all term. I swear, you could see your future in it – if your future involved a very clear, possibly flammable liquid.

Leo squinted at it, then grinned. "Man, that looks like some serious *moonshine*."

And he was right. It did. Not that I've ever seen actual moonshine, mind you, but every movie cliché of some backwoods distiller proudly displaying his crystal-clear, illicit spirit suddenly made sense. Here we were, in a sterile Caltech lab, achieving the same optical clarity and chemical potency (minus the illicit part, thankfully). It got me thinking about the whole history of it – basic chemistry applied in ingenious, if sometimes illegal, ways. The pursuit of purity, whether for science or... other purposes.

It’s kind of wild, isn’t it? The same principles we’re learning here, the same careful application of heat and phase changes, are at the heart of so many things, from pharmaceuticals to… well, *moonshine*. My brain's a bit too fried to delve into a deep philosophical treatise on the intersection of chemistry and folklore right now, though. I still have that monstrous P-set for CS 101 looming. My code for the sorting algorithm is looking less like elegant logic and more like pure, unadulterated chaos.

Maybe if I stare at that clear distillate for long enough, it’ll give me some clarity on my buggy code. Or maybe I just need to get some sleep. Probably the latter.

-Trevor
