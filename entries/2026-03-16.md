Dear diary,

Ugh, another Monday. Started with Calc III, which always feels like trying to wrestle an octopus while simultaneously solving a Rubik's Cube. My brain felt like it was doing Fourier transforms just to process breakfast.

But then things got… interesting. In Phys 103 lab, we were setting up the optical bench to measure diffraction patterns – pretty standard stuff, light sources, gratings, detectors – when someone discovered a rather vibrant, hot pink *thong* draped quite artfully over one of the laser mounts. Not even subtly, just prominently displayed.

The lab went silent for a beat. Professor Chen walked over, adjusted his glasses, and stared at it for a good ten seconds without a word. Then he just deadpanned, "Well, it certainly adds an… unexpected element to our light absorption and reflection study, wouldn't you say?" The entire lab burst out laughing.

No one claimed it, of course. Definitely a hack. Probably some elaborate pre-Decompression prank from the seniors. Later, I swear I saw it again, fluttering from the beak of one of the Athenaeum gargoyles. The sheer dedication to absurdity here never ceases to amaze me. I just hope it doesn't end up integrated into my orbital mechanics problem set tomorrow. My brain can only handle so much chaos before it starts trying to calculate the tensile strength of novelty undergarments.

-Trevor
