Dear diary,

Wednesday, June 3rd, 2026. What a day. What a week. What a *term*. The clock finally hit 11:59 PM, and I hit 'submit' on the CS1 final project for the last time. My brain feels less like a functional neural network and more like a fragmented hard drive stuck in an endless defrag loop, or maybe just permanently showing the spinning wheel of death.

The project itself was… ambitious. We were trying to build a sentiment analysis model for historical Caltech student reviews – figuring out if previous generations were just as perpetually stressed as we are. The actual coding, while challenging, felt almost secondary to the sheer amount of time spent just *waiting*. Waiting for the enormous dataset of scanned documents and transcribed comments to finish **loading** into memory. Waiting for the pre-trained language model weights to download and **load**. Waiting for the Python environment to correctly **load** all the obscure dependencies that only worked on specific versions that only *I* seemed to have trouble with.

There were moments I swore I could actually *feel* my GPU coil whine from exhaustion as it tried to process the data, constantly showing me a progress bar that barely moved, or worse, just a blank screen where I *knew* something was **loading** but offered no visual feedback. It felt like my life was one giant **loading** screen, complete with generic background music (usually the drone of the ventilation system in the library). My mental state mirrored the process: trying to **load** new concepts from lectures, debugging obscure errors, and then patiently, painstakingly, **loading** the fixed code back into the system for another run.

Professor Lee kept telling us to "trust the process," but it's hard to trust a process when it's taking three hours to **load** a file you need to debug. I almost started talking to the progress bars. "Are you **loading**, buddy? Are you doing okay? Need some more RAM? I can share some coffee, maybe that'll speed things up."

But it's done. Submitted. The relief is palpable, like finally exiting a perpetually buffering video. My brain, which has been trying to **load** a semester's worth of CS knowledge and three other classes' worth of information, finally feels like it can offload some of that data. I'm looking forward to a summer of hopefully less intense **loading** – maybe just **loading** up on sleep, **loading** my plate with some decent food that isn't from the vending machine, and perhaps **loading** a game that *actually* lets me play instead of just watching a progress bar.

It’s funny, even as I type this, I can almost hear the faint whirring of some server somewhere, endlessly **loading** data for someone else’s project. But for now, I'm just going to focus on **loading** myself into bed.

-Trevor
