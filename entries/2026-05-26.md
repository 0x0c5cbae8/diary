It’s such a stark contrast to the sterile, late-night hum of the computers in our dorm and the frantic scratching of pens on whiteboards. My grandmother, who can barely operate a smartphone, somehow manages to provide a level of grounding that my triple-integral-solving brain often misses.

It’s funny, isn't it? Here I am, trying to understand the fundamental particles of the universe, and it’s a simple, home-baked cookie from my grandmother that reminds me of simpler, more fundamental truths – like the importance of sleep, food, and the people who care about you. For a few minutes, I wasn't just Trevor, the stressed-out Caltech freshman. I was just "sweet pea," and the universe suddenly felt a little less intimidating.

Okay, back to the p-set. The shortbread provided a much-needed energy boost and, more importantly, a little mental reset. Maybe I can finally figure out this eigenvalue. Thanks, Grandma.
