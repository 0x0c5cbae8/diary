Dear diary,

It's Saturday. March 21st, 2026. And my brain still hasn't quite processed that it's *not* Tuesday, with a Ma 2c P-set deadline looming, or a Ph 1c lab report needing just "one more look." Finals week for winter term was… a blur. Pretty sure I ran solely on cold brew and the faint hope of a decent GPA. I even saw the sun for a few minutes today, which felt like a minor miracle.

Anyway, I was grabbing some late lunch at Chandler – mostly to avoid the overwhelming silence of my room after a week of frantic typing – and I overheard some sophomores talking, very animatedly, about "declaration forms." Declaring their majors. And suddenly, my brief moment of post-finals bliss evaporated, replaced by a fresh wave of existential dread.

I mean, I'm just a freshman. I've barely survived two terms of core curriculum. Physics, math, chemistry, computation… it's all fascinating, and it's all brutally hard. How am I supposed to *declare* one thing? It feels like choosing your life's path before you've even figured out how to consistently beat the laundry room rush on a Sunday evening.

One minute I'm convinced I'm destined for Theoretical Physics, grappling with the elegant brutality of quantum mechanics and the mind-bending concepts of relativity. The next, I'm thinking about bioengineering, picturing myself designing some microscopic robot to fix cells, or optimizing genetic sequences. Or maybe computation and AI, because coding those complex simulations, despite the bugs, is oddly satisfying. Every P-set, every lab, every late-night discussion, it's like a siren song pulling me in a slightly different direction. And everyone here is so damn *good* at everything, it just amplifies the pressure. Like, am I good enough to declare *this* over *that*? Will I be good enough *period*?

I haven't even *begun* to think seriously about research yet, which everyone keeps telling me is crucial for choosing a major. It's like I need a *declaration of intent* to figure out my actual declaration of major. A promise to myself that I won't drown in the options, that I'll somehow synthesize this chaotic intellectual buffet into a coherent, sustainable path.

So, I guess I'll make a smaller, more immediate declaration tonight. I declare that I will spend at least one hour *not* thinking about academic choices, future careers, or the crushing weight of expectation. Maybe I'll finally dive into that sci-fi novel I started months ago. Or maybe I'll just stare blankly at the ceiling and let my brain defragment. And I declare that next term, I *will* try to get more than five hours of sleep on average. It's ambitious, I know, but a man can dream, right? The P-sets will return soon enough. But for tonight, the declaration of "no more thinking" is my only priority. My head already hurts from thinking about thinking.

-Trevor
