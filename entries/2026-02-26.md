Dear diary,

It's Thursday night, or rather, Friday morning if you look at the clock. My brain feels like it's been run through a particle accelerator and then gently (or not so gently) deconstructed. PSET 7 for Ph 120 (Quantum Mechanics) has been my nemesis for the past 48 hours. Specifically, problem 3. It involved some really nasty perturbation theory, and for the life of me, I couldn't get the integrals to converge into anything sensible.

It was like there was this **curtain** in my mind, a thick, velvet one, hanging directly between me and the solution. I understood the concepts in class, I could re-derive the formulas, but when it came to *applying* them to this particular system, it was just a blank wall. Every time I thought I had an angle, I’d hit another algebraic dead-end or a physical interpretation that just didn't make sense. I even tried explaining it out loud to the rubber duck on my desk, which usually helps, but even Quackers seemed stumped tonight.

I was about to give up, push it off until 3 AM coffee, when I decided to take one last look at the initial conditions, just *one more time*. And that's when it hit me. A subtle wording in the problem statement, something I'd skimmed over a dozen times, completely changed the boundary conditions for one of the potentials. It wasn't a standard infinite square well with a perturbation as I'd assumed; it was slightly different, subtly asymmetric.

And just like that, the **curtain** wasn't just pulled aside; it dissolved. The entire problem, which had seemed like an insurmountable fortress, revealed itself as an elegant sequence of logical steps. The integrals, once monstrous, simplified beautifully. It was almost… *obvious*. The sheer elegance of it, once I saw past my own preconceived notions, was breathtaking. That feeling, when a complex theoretical concept suddenly *clicks* and you see the underlying beauty, it’s why I came to Caltech. Even if it requires hours of head-desking first.

Now it's done. Or at least, Problem 3 is done. Only two more to go before I can even *think* about sleep. At least the actual curtain in my room is thin enough that I can just barely see the faintest hint of pre-dawn light trying to peek through. Maybe I'll get around to buying a proper blackout one this quarter. Or maybe I'll just learn to sleep through the sun.

-Trevor
