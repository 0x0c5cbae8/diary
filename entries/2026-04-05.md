Dear diary,

Another Sunday vanishes into the ether. Just when I think I might catch up, another P-set manifests out of thin air. Today was a strange mix of productive and utterly chaotic, which, I suppose, is peak Caltech.

Spent most of the afternoon in the machine shop with Maya and Chen, trying to get our 'Autonomous Projectile Delivery System' (aka, a fancy catapult) ready for the ME 105 demo on Tuesday. The assignment is deceptively simple: build a device that can accurately *fling* a standard tennis ball into a designated target zone from varying distances. Simple in theory, soul-crushing in practice.

We'd been fine-tuning the tension springs for hours. The first few tests were... dramatic. Our initial prototype, bless its heart, actually *flung* the ball straight up into the ceiling girders, where it got stuck for a good ten minutes before we managed to dislodge it with a very long broom. Chen nearly got decapitated when the arm recoiled. Good times.

Finally, though, after adjusting the release mechanism and recalibrating the launch angle, we managed a few decent shots. It’s still not perfect, but at least the ball is landing *somewhere* near the target now, instead of being wildly *flung* into the adjacent bay. There’s a strange satisfaction in seeing a physical object obey your carefully calculated parameters, even if it took three failed designs and nearly all our sanity to get there.

After that, I stumbled back to Fleming, feeling utterly spent. Just *flung* my backpack onto my desk, barely noticing the stack of unread textbooks threatening to collapse. Sometimes I feel like freshman year itself is one giant experiment in how much a human being can take before they get *flung* completely off course. It’s a constant battle to stay on track, to not get lost in the endless stream of lectures, labs, and late-night debugging sessions.

I guess I'm lucky I landed at Caltech, even if it feels like I was *flung* headfirst into a whirlwind. It's challenging, yes, but also incredibly exhilarating. I wouldn't trade it, even for a moment of quiet where the only thing being flung is a frisbee on the Olive Walk, not my GPA.

Gotta try to get some sleep before another week of being an engineering guinea pig.

-Trevor
