Dear diary,

Tuesday. March 3rd, 2026. Another Tuesday. You know, sometimes it feels like the universe just decides to pile up all the hardest p-sets for one specific day of the week, and for me, that day is definitely Tuesday. Differential Equations p-set due at midnight, a ridiculously long problem from my Frosh Physics lab that required a *lot* more Excel-ing than I anticipated, and a quick turnaround on a Chem quiz review for tomorrow. My brain feels like it’s been put through a centrifuge.

But, amidst the glorious chaos that is Caltech freshman year, there was a beacon of hope tonight: the House Chili Cook-off. Seriously, our Ricketts house social chairs are geniuses. Just when everyone was about to hit peak stress-induced delirium, they announced it last week, and tonight was the night. I almost didn't go, buried as I was under equations, but the smell wafting from the lounge was just too tempting.

And oh my god, the chili! People actually *went all out*. There was everything from a classic, hearty beef chili that tasted like someone's grandma made it, to a vegan sweet potato and black bean concoction that was surprisingly good, to something I swear had Ghost Peppers in it (I only took a tiny, brave spoon and my mouth is still tingling). Someone even tried to make a "chemically optimized" chili, apparently calculating the perfect capsaicin-to-bean ratio. It tasted... interesting. Definitely unique.

I didn't enter, obviously, because my culinary skills currently extend to making instant ramen and *maybe* boiling water without burning it. But just being there, spooning out different samples, arguing with Sarah from upstairs about whether a proper chili *must* have beans (it totally does, she's wrong), and just generally *not* thinking about Hamiltonian mechanics was exactly what I needed. The winner was surprisingly the one with dark chocolate and coffee in it – super rich and complex. Definitely gave me some ideas for future attempts if I ever find time to learn how to cook something beyond basic survival.

It’s crazy how these little things, these random, slightly chaotic house events, are what keep me sane sometimes. Knowing that everyone else is also in the trenches with their p-sets, but we can all take an hour to just *be* and eat some excellent chili together, really makes the never-ending grind feel a little more manageable. My stomach is full, my brain is slightly less fried, and I actually feel a little ready to tackle the rest of that Diff Eq problem. Maybe. After a short break to let the chili settle.

-Trevor
