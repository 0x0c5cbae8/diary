Dear diary,

Followed that up with my Physics 11 lecture – today was all about quantum entanglement, which is both fascinating and deeply disturbing when you try to actually *understand* it. My brain feels like it’s been put through a cosmic blender.

After a quick, sad lunch (the dining hall's "mystery meat" Tuesday special), I spent the afternoon debugging my circuit for EE 1. My breadboard looks like a tangled mess of spaghetti, and at one point I was convinced I’d somehow created a tiny black hole that was just sucking up all the current. Turns out, I just had a resistor in backwards. Classic Trevor.

But here’s the thing, diary. All day, in the back of my mind, there was this little voice. A persistent, slightly annoying little voice, whispering, "Trevor, remember your New Year's resolution? Remember 'physical well-being'?" Yes, it's May. Yes, the resolution died sometime in late January when the first round of midterms hit. But today, for some reason, the guilt was extra strong.

I had *planned* to go to the **gym**. Seriously! I even packed my ancient gym shorts and a t-shirt that's probably older than me. My intention was noble: a solid 30 minutes on the elliptical, maybe a few reps with the 5-pound dumbbells (don't judge, it's a start), just to clear my head before diving into my humanities essay due tomorrow.

And then I remembered I had to email Professor Schmidt about that weird error in the lab report, which led to me getting sucked into a wormhole of research papers on obscure material science. Next thing I knew, it was 8 PM, I was still in my day clothes, and my gym bag was giving me the evil eye from the corner of my room.

I saw a few actual buff people heading out of the Braun Athletic Center earlier, all sweaty and looking energized. I just looked sweaty and defeated from debugging.

Maybe tomorrow, diary. Maybe tomorrow the P-sets will be less demanding, the code will compile on the first try, and the allure of a brief escape to the **gym** won't be overshadowed by the gravitational pull of deadlines. Or maybe I’ll just convince myself that walking to the vending machine for a late-night snack is sufficient cardio. A Caltech student's gotta make do, right?

Time to face that humanities essay. Wish me luck.
