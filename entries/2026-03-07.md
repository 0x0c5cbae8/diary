Dear diary,

Saturday. What even *is* a Saturday at Caltech? Feels more like "Friday Part 2: The P-Set Strikes Back." I swear, I spent most of today just staring at my monitor, trying to coax my Python script into behaving. We’ve got this absurdly complex simulation for CS2, and my code kept getting stuck in some recursive loop, refusing to terminate. It was driving me absolutely bonkers. Every time I ran it, it would just hang there, an endless procession of `print` statements, never reaching a clean **exit**. I must have tried a dozen different debugging strategies. Added more print statements, commented out entire functions, even tried to draw out the call stack on my whiteboard – which, let's be honest, looked more like a spaghetti monster by the end.

I was getting seriously frustrated. I almost threw in the towel and just left the computer lab to find some actual human interaction, to just *exit* the confines of this problem for a bit. There was a dodgeball game happening on the Olive Walk, and I could hear the shouts and laughter drifting up. For a moment, I just wanted to forget about it, just walk out and join them. But then, a thought sparked: what if the edge case I was missing wasn't in the calculation, but in the *conditions* for the loop's termination?

And boom. Five minutes later, after tweaking a single `if` statement, it ran. Smoothly. All the way to a clean **exit**. The final output appeared, correct, pristine. The relief was immense. It was like the entire room just exhaled with me. Seriously, I might have cheered a little too loudly for a public computer lab. Now I finally understand why Professor Schmidt keeps harping on about graceful termination. It's not just good practice; it's a mental health requirement.

After that, I finally *did* manage to exit the lab and grab some late dinner with Maya and Ben. We debated the merits of different pizza toppings for an hour, which felt like a much-needed break from debugging abstract data structures. Still, even over pizza, I found myself replaying the bug in my head, thinking about how close I was to giving up. It's funny how a single `exit` command can represent so much: the end of a process, the relief of a solution, or sometimes, the desperate act of bailing out. Today, it was definitely the former, and I'm really glad I stuck with it.

Now, if only I could find a graceful **exit** strategy from the mountain of laundry I have waiting. That might be an even tougher problem.

-Trevor
