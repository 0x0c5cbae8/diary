Dear diary,

Monday. Already feels like a Tuesday, or maybe next Friday, just from the sheer chronological distortion that is Caltech life. Physics 1c p-set due today, and I swear Gauss's Law has a personal vendetta against my sleep schedule. But that wasn't even the main event.

The highlight – or lowlight, depending on how much sleep I got last night, which was minimal – was our final EM lab project. We’re building this rudimentary radio receiver, and let me tell you, it's 90% fiddly wiring and 10% praying to Maxwell. Today’s specific torture involved securing the antenna coil to its base. The instructions were vague, as usual: "ensure stable contact."

"Stable contact" apparently translates to "spend twenty minutes carefully winding, then *taping* the delicate enamel-coated wire to a small plastic frame without snapping it, crushing it, or inadvertently shorting something." I must have gone through half a roll of electrical *tape* alone. First, a thin strip to hold the initial winding. Then another, tighter strip, making sure not to apply too much pressure. Then, when a critical connection kept wiggling, I had to fashion a little strain relief using yet *another* piece of *tape*. It's absurd, really. You spend hours learning about electromagnetic fields, complex impedances, and signal-to-noise ratios, only to have the success of your entire endeavor hinge on a bit of sticky plastic.

My lab partner, Maya, was laughing as I meticulously applied another layer, declaring, "Trevor, you're becoming a *tape* artisan." I just grunted. But you know, it’s true. Every segment, every overlap, had to be just right. If it wasn't strong enough, the coil would sag. Too tight, and the enamel would scrape, causing a short. It was a delicate dance of adhesion and tension. By the time we finally got the faint static of a local AM station, I felt less like an engineer and more like a professional *tape* applicator.

We submitted the report, probably covered in microscopic *tape* residue, and now it's back to the grind. Organic Chem midterm next week. I need to get some sleep, but first, there's that Math 1b p-set that's staring me down. Sometimes I think Caltech is just an elaborate test of how many different kinds of *tape* you can master before you graduate.

Wish me luck finding my own 'stable contact' with my bed tonight.

-Trevor
