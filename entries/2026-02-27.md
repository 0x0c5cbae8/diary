Dear diary,

Finally, Friday. Seriously, I don’t think I’ve ever been so glad to see 5 PM. I just turned in the Ma 1a p-set, and my brain feels like it’s been put through a cheese grater. I swear, Professor Feynman’s ghost must be laughing at us from beyond, thinking this is all too easy.

After that marathon, I needed a break, so I put on one of those historical engineering documentaries. This one was about the Panama Canal. It's mind-boggling to think about the sheer *scale* of human effort and ingenuity that went into it. The terrain, the diseases, the engineering challenges – it makes my p-sets seem trivial by comparison.

What really struck me was the sheer number of people involved, the coordination, the relentless dedication over decades. They had to organize a whole *corps* of workers, engineers, doctors, logistics teams – it wasn't just a few brilliant minds, but a massive collective effort, solving problems that no single person could have tackled alone. It was more than just a workforce; it was a disciplined, problem-solving machine.

It got me thinking about us here at Caltech. Sometimes it feels like we’re a small *corps* ourselves, just a bunch of freshmen, thrown into the deep end of the academic pool, learning to tackle seemingly impossible problems together. We’re not digging canals, but we're wrestling with abstract concepts that feel just as monumental at times. Maybe one day, a *corps* of us will be building the next generation of space telescopes or solving climate change. The thought is both daunting and incredibly exciting.

Anyway, my brain is officially done for the week. Time for some actual sleep, or maybe just staring blankly at the ceiling for a while.

-Trevor
