Dear diary,

Tuesday. Another one down. Feels like the week just started and already my brain feels like it’s run a marathon. We had that PSet due for CS 1 this morning – the one with the really fiddly algorithms. I spent half the night wrestling with a particularly stubborn bug. I swear, the first few weeks here, everything felt like I was trying to decipher an alien language. Every problem, every concept, just utterly *unfamiliar*.

But today, something clicked. While debugging that code, I realized I wasn't just blindly trying things anymore. I was following a pattern, checking for common pitfalls, almost instinctively knowing where to look. It’s not that the problems are *easy* now – far from it – but there's a growing sense of *familiarity* with the process, the way Caltech expects you to think. The language of logic, the debugging steps… it’s slowly becoming a part of my internal monologue.

It’s the same with campus, I guess. When I first got here, I was constantly checking Google Maps to get from Fleming to Ramo, or even just finding the right dining hall. Now, I navigate the labyrinthine tunnels and the various shortcuts without a second thought. The faces in the common room, the professors’ quirks, even the smell of overly-caffeinated despair wafting from the library late at night – it all feels so *familiar* now.

Sometimes I miss the familiarity of home, of course. My mom’s cooking, my old room, the comfort of knowing exactly what to expect from my day. But there’s a different kind of comfort blooming here, a new familiarity forged in shared late nights and mutual struggle. It's the familiarity of knowing I'm not alone in the grind, that the person next to me in the library is probably also questioning their life choices over a P-set.

It’s funny how something so relentlessly challenging can slowly transform into something you’re intimately familiar with. Maybe that’s the trick of Caltech: they throw you into the deep end until you either sink or start swimming, and eventually, the water itself starts to feel like an old friend.

Anyway, gotta crash. Big Physics lecture tomorrow. Hopefully, some of the concepts there will start feeling a little more *familiar* too. One can dream.

-Trevor
