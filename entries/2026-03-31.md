Dear diary,

Tuesday, March 31, 2026. Another Tuesday, another existential crisis fueled by caffeine and the sheer terror of quantum mechanics. I swear, I thought high school was hard, but Caltech manages to find new and exciting ways to make my brain feel like it’s trying to exit my skull. Ph 12a is eating me alive, and then there’s CS 1, and the lab for Ch 2a that decided to spontaneously combust today (metaphorically, thank goodness, but my yield was 0.003% which feels like a scientific tragedy).

I’ve been holed up in my room in Avery since dinner, trying to debug a circuit for this CS project. It's supposed to detect subtle voltage fluctuations, but right now it's mostly detecting my rapidly dwindling sanity. The clock on my desktop just flickered to 2:47 AM. Sleep? What's sleep? I'm pretty sure it's a mythical creature I only hear about in stories from pre-frosh summer programs.

I rubbed my eyes, probably for the thousandth time tonight, trying to scrub away the blur of schematics and terminal windows. When I pulled my hand away, there it was, clinging stubbornly to my finger. A tiny, dark curve – one of my own **eyelash**es.

For a split second, my brain wanted to analyze its keratin structure, its approximate length-to-width ratio, maybe even how many mites might be living on it (don't ask, the internet tells you things). But then the sleep-deprived, utterly desperate part of me took over. The part that's clinging to any shred of hope, however illogical, in this highly logical institution.

I carefully placed the tiny lash on my thumb, took a deep breath, and wished with all my might. Not for an A, or even for an extra hour of sleep. I wished for the circuit diagram for Problem 4 on the Ph 12a problem set to *finally* make sense, and for my CS code to compile without a thousand errors. I actually blew on the eyelash for good measure before flicking it away.

It felt ridiculous, a Caltech freshman making a wish on an eyelash. But honestly, at this point, I'll take any help I can get. Maybe the universe will pity my exhausted state. Or maybe it's just another symptom of my rapidly approaching burnout.

Back to the circuit. Wish me luck. Or, better yet, wish me sleep.

-Trevor
