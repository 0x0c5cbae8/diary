Dear diary,

It's Friday. June 12th. And I can officially, definitively, irrevocably say that *spring quarter is OVER*. My last final, EE 21: Digital Systems, was this morning. My brain feels like it’s been put through a meat grinder and then reassembled incorrectly. The whole final felt like Professor Davies was just sitting there, a silent, all-knowing *lord* of logic gates and state machines, observing our frantic attempts to recall the difference between a Mealy and a Moore machine.

I honestly thought I was going to combust when I hit submit. My hands were shaking. I haven't slept more than four hours a night all week, and the instant coffee has stopped working. I'm pretty sure my blood type is now 80% caffeine and 20% existential dread. *Lord* help me if I have to look at another Karnaugh map for the next three months. My eyes glaze over just thinking about it.

But it’s done. It’s finally, truly done. For the first time in what feels like forever, I don't have a PSet looming over me, a lab report to write, or a final project to debug. I can just... *be*. I might actually attempt to sleep for twelve hours straight. And maybe even eat something that wasn't prepared in a microwave.

Tomorrow, a bunch of us are planning to just crash at the beach, assuming we can actually coordinate our schedules and find a ride. It sounds glorious. Like being reborn, away from the watchful, omniscient gaze of the academic *lords* who control our grades.

I still have nightmares about the Orgo final. *Lord*, that was rough.

-Trevor
