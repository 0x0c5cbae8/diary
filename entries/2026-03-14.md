Dear diary,

Happy Pi Day! Or at least, as happy as it gets when you're technically celebrating the mathematical constant on a Saturday by... well, you'll see. It's 3.14, so naturally, the entire campus smells faintly of pie and existential dread from the upcoming midterms. Today was supposed to be a reprieve, a day of glorious, carb-filled celebration. For some of us, it was also a day of minor, albeit hilarious, legal repercussions.

We were having a blast, naturally. The usual Pi Day pie-eating contest (I strategically avoided that sugar rush), the reciting of digits (got to 150 before I choked, shame), and of course, the House pranks. That's where things got… interesting.

See, Fleming House tried to 'borrow' our inflatable π symbol from the Dabney courtyard, claiming 'numerical domain' and leaving behind a cryptic note about Euler's Identity. Being intrepid Dabneyites, a few of us freshmen, myself included, decided a counter-retrieval was in order. It was all in good fun, mostly. We managed to liberate our π from their common room, but as we were making our getaway across the Olive Walk, we got cornered near Beckman Auditorium by a couple of incredibly fast and surprisingly strong Fleming seniors.

They didn't just take the π back, oh no. They held an impromptu 'trial' right there, under the stars, with a flashlight acting as the gavel. The 'charge'? 'Unsanctioned π-terruption of Pi Day festivities' and 'Theft of a Numerically Significant Inflatable.' Seriously, their theatrics were impressive, even if I was the principal defendant. And then came the… *sentencing*.

My punishment? I wasn't just *sentenced* to give back their inflatable. Oh no. I was *sentenced* to help them bake *extra* pies for the undergrads who missed out earlier tonight, *and* I had to explain the proof for the irrationality of π to their confused freshman mascot (who was dressed as a giant pigeon, naturally) for half an hour. It wasn't harsh, but it was certainly humbling, and a bit humiliating having to explain complex math to a guy in a giant bird suit who kept asking if π was "fluffy."

Honestly, it was actually kind of fun, in a weird Caltech way. The upperclassmen were laughing, I learned how to make a surprisingly decent apple pie (who knew I had it in me?), and the mascot actually seemed to grasp some of the concepts by the end, or at least he stopped flapping his wings aimlessly. It's moments like these that make Caltech, well, Caltech. Even when you're getting metaphorically 'sentenced' for minor Pi Day espionage. Now, if you'll excuse me, I think I smell residual pie fumes clinging to my clothes. Time for some much-needed sleep before tomorrow’s organic chemistry problem set sentences me to another kind of punishment.

-Trevor
