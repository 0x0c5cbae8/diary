Dear diary,

Another Sunday bleeds into Monday, and I swear the sun sets faster on these days just to mock our impending doom. Just finished (mostly) the P-set for CS 1.1, and my brain feels like it’s been put through a quantum entanglement machine with a particularly stubborn pile of laundry. Now onto Bio 1b. Professor Albright wants us to pick a non-human primate and analyze their social learning behaviors. I chose orangutans. Specifically, their incredible tool use and cultural transmission of foraging techniques.

It's fascinating, don't get me wrong. Reading about them using leaves as napkins, or sticks to extract insects... it’s mind-blowing how intelligent they are. But trying to synthesize all this research into a coherent, Caltech-level paper after staring at Python code for six hours? My brain cells are staging a sit-in. I swear I'm starting to mimic their posture, slumped over my desk, reaching for another lukewarm cup of coffee like it’s a particularly elusive termite mound.

I took a quick sanity break, wandering down to the common room for some water, and nearly tripped over... well, not a real orangutan, thankfully. But someone from Fleming must have decided to "enhance" our lounge. There, perched precariously on top of the old, slightly-too-high bookshelf, was one of those life-sized plush orangutans. The kind you win at a ridiculously rigged carnival game. Its wise, saggy face seemed to be staring directly at my laptop, as if silently judging my progress on its species' behavioral patterns.

It was so absurd, so perfectly Caltech, that I actually laughed out loud. A deep, slightly hysterical laugh. Here I am, deep-diving into the complex cognitive abilities of *Pongo pygmaeus*, and a giant, fluffy, inanimate representation of one is silently mocking me from across the room. It was a weirdly comforting moment though, a reminder that even amidst the intensity, there's always something utterly bizarre and wonderful happening on campus.

Maybe it's a good omen. Or maybe I just need more sleep. Definitely more sleep. Off to try and decode more primatology papers before the real week hits. Wish me luck.

-Trevor
