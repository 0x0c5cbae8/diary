Dear diary,

Another Sunday bleeds into Monday, as it always does here. Just submitted the EM-3 P-set, and my brain feels like it’s been run through a particle accelerator. I swear, the magnetic fields are starting to induce actual hallucinations. I saw a derivation for Ampere's law written in mashed potatoes on my plate at Chandler earlier.

But the real struggle tonight wasn't electromagnetism, it was the cursed **Raspberry Pi** I’m trying to get working for the upcoming Hackathon. I’m attempting to build a 'smart plant watering system' for our house common room's sad-looking ficus. It started as a noble quest to save a botanical life, but now it feels like the Pi itself is sentient and actively mocking my Python scripts. For three hours, I've been wrestling with a GPIO pin that just refuses to output correctly. I've re-soldered, re-flashed, re-coded, and even tried talking to it nicely. Nothing.

It's ironic, isn't it? Something named after such a sweet, simple fruit – a raspberry – causing so much bitter agony. I keep thinking, how hard can it be? It's just a tiny computer. But then I remember it's *me* trying to program it after a week of sleep deprivation and enough caffeine to power a small village. Maybe the ficus is beyond saving anyway. Or maybe the problem is that I'm just missing some tiny, obvious logic error. It feels like trying to find a single specific *berry* in a whole field of similar ones, all while blindfolded and being poked by a very persistent quantum mechanic.

I finally threw in the towel for tonight. My eyes are burning, and I can almost hear the little Pi laughing at me from my desk. Tomorrow, fresh eyes. Maybe the Pi will feel merciful after a night's rest. Or maybe I'll just buy the ficus a self-watering pot from Amazon. It would probably be cheaper and less emotionally damaging.

Goodnight, or rather, good morning.

-Trevor
