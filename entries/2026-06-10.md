Dear diary,

It's Wednesday, June 10th, 2026. And for the first time in what feels like forever, I can actually breathe. Finals are *done*. Physics 1b, Ma 1b, CS 1, Ph 2, even that crazy Hum 1c paper on medieval philosophy – all submitted, all over. My brain feels like it's been wrung out, dried, and then re-folded incorrectly. But, God, the relief is glorious.

I ended up down by the reflecting pond tonight. It's late, probably past 1 AM, but the air is still warm, and the campus is eerily quiet now that everyone’s either packed or celebrating. The moon's reflecting off the water, making the surface look like a sheet of hammered silver. I just sat on one of those benches, staring into it.

It made me think about the whole "big fish in a small pond" thing. Back in high school, I was definitely a big fish. Straight A's, aced all the APs, captain of the robotics team. My local pond, you know? It felt comfortable, predictable. I knew every current, every rock.

Then I came here. And Caltech… Caltech isn't just a bigger pond, it's like a whole new ocean. Or, no, it's still a pond, but it's one of those ridiculously deep, ancient, bioluminescent ones you find in underwater caves, full of creatures I never even knew existed. All these incredible, brilliant people, each one a different kind of magnificent, complex organism. Some are elegant swimmers, some are bottom-feeders just grinding away, some are predators of knowledge, and a few are just weird glowing things doing their own fascinating stuff. I spent this whole freshman year feeling like a tiny little minnow, darting around, trying not to get eaten, trying to figure out how to even *survive* in this ridiculously intense ecosystem.

It was humbling, to say the least. There were days I felt completely lost, like I’d forgotten how to even swim. But tonight, looking at the water, I realized something. Even though I might still feel like a minnow compared to some of the postdocs or even the upperclassmen, I'm not the same minnow who arrived last September. I've learned to navigate these deep currents, to avoid the scary-smart sharks, and I've even found some other minnows to school with. I’ve grown fins I didn’t know I had.

This pond, this Caltech pond, it’s shaped me. And while I’m ready for a break – maybe a literal dip in an actual, much shallower pond somewhere – I’m also kind of excited to see what else I can discover in its depths next year.

-Trevor
