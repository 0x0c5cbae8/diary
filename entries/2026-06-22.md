Dear diary,

It's officially Monday, June 22nd, and I'm still feeling the residual brain-fry from finals week. My brain feels like a badly defragmented hard drive. The grades for my last two classes finally dropped late last night – Physics 11 wasn't as bad as I thought, but I definitely almost shorted out my laptop trying to submit the last CS project. Phew.

Now, the real waiting game begins. The SURF decision emails were supposed to start trickling out today. I’ve probably refreshed my inbox a hundred times already. Every new notification, every little chime, sends my heart racing. I click on it, scan the 'sender' field... and it's always just another campus announcement about library hours or a reminder to return my overdue textbooks. Not the one I’m desperate for.

I mean, this SURF project – building that neural network for material science – is everything. Dr. Sharma's lab is *the* place to be. If I don't get this, my summer plans are... well, 'plans' might be too strong a word. More like 'existential dread and maybe some sad coding tutorials on YouTube'. I even briefly considered checking the spam folder, just in case the universe decided to play a cruel joke and filter out the most important email of my freshman year. But no, nothing from a 'Caltech Summer Undergraduate Research Fellowships Office' sender in there.

It's agonizing. The thought that the decision has already been made, and that email is just floating somewhere in the ether, waiting to be dispatched by some server... it's a test of patience that even quantum mechanics problem sets didn't prepare me for. I just need that definitive word, that name in the sender line, so I can finally breathe.

Just going to keep staring at that inbox. Hope the right 'sender' decides to make an appearance soon.

-Trevor
