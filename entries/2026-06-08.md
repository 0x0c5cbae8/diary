Dear diary,

Monday, June 8, 2026

My brain feels like a deep-fried circuit board after finals. I swear I can still hear Professor Schmidt's voice droning about statistical mechanics in my sleep. But hey, it's done. Freshman year, officially *done*. I survived. Barely.

Well, almost. Technically, I'm already supposed to be "transitioning" into my summer UROP. Dr. Chen sent an email late last night with a list of papers to skim and some preliminary Python scripts to get familiar with. My initial reaction was pure dread. More reading? More coding? My brain cells are staging a walk-out and demanding severance pay.

But as I finally dragged myself to my desk this morning, a funny thought struck me. This little pre-research assignment, this "skimming of foundational literature" and "familiarization with existing code," it's like an academic *aperitif*. Not a fancy gin and tonic before a Michelin-star meal, obviously – unless "sad instant ramen" counts as a Michelin meal and "desperate cramming" counts as a cocktail. But an aperitif is meant to stimulate the appetite, right? To get the intellectual juices flowing before the main course of actual, hands-on research hits.

And weirdly, it's working. Reading that first paper, "On the Anomalous Diffusion of Chaotic Systems," felt less like a chore and more like… a palate cleanser. A little sip of something new and intriguing, a gentle warm-up for the brain before I dive headfirst into eight weeks of trying not to break Dr. Chen's particle accelerator simulations. Or at least, I *hope* it's a gentle warm-up. Knowing Caltech, it's probably more like a shot of espresso directly to the frontal lobe.

I guess this aperitif is working because I'm actually starting to feel a flicker of excitement, or at least a manageable level of anxiety, about the summer. Freshman year was an absolute sprint, a constant deluge of new information. Maybe this summer, this UROP, will be a chance to really dig deep into one thing, to contribute to something tangible. Or at least, to finally get a decent amount of sleep before second year starts.

Wish me luck on my aperitif, and the very substantial meal to follow.

-Trevor
