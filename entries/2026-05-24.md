Dear diary,

May 24, 2026. Another Sunday, another existential crisis disguised as a problem set. Or maybe it’s the other way around. I honestly can’t tell anymore. Spring quarter is really grinding me down, and the finish line for freshman year feels both impossibly close and infinitely far away. My brain currently feels like a neural network that's been force-fed too many conflicting datasets.

Today’s major hurdle, however, wasn't a quantum mechanics derivation or debugging my Python code for the astrophysics project. No, today's Everest was far more insidious, far more *inconvenient*. I needed to print out the cover sheet for my CS 1 problem set – just one page, mind you, one measly piece of paper to declare my glorious (and mostly finished, thank god) work.

I walked down to the common room printer, humming a tune from a forgotten anime, already mentally celebrating getting this one small thing done. And then, the horror. "Paper Tray Empty." Seriously? At 10 PM on a Sunday? I checked the usual stash. Nothing. Absolutely bone dry. I swear, the universe conspires against me with such mundane cruelty sometimes. It’s not enough that I spent eight hours wrestling with recursive algorithms; now I have to go on a scavenger hunt for printer paper.

My first thought was, "Fine, I'll just run to the library." But then the logistics kicked in. It's a five-minute walk each way, minimum. Five minutes I don't *have* when I could be using that time to re-check a calculation, or, you know, actually sleep. Plus, the library printers are always a gamble – one might be out of toner, another might decide it doesn't recognize my ID today. It's the cumulative mental load of anticipating these minor potential failures that really gets to me.

I ended up having to walk to Fleming House to borrow some paper from Dave (who, bless his sleep-deprived soul, actually had some). The whole ordeal took fifteen minutes. Fifteen minutes! In the grand scheme of things, it’s nothing. A blip. A non-event. But after a week of 3 AM bedtimes and lectures that feel like cognitive assaults, these little bumps in the road feel like insurmountable obstacles. It’s the definition of an *inconvenience*, isn't it? A minor obstruction that nonetheless derails your carefully orchestrated, already precarious schedule and drains precious mental energy.

I just want to print a single page without having to engage in a minor quest. Is that too much to ask? Probably, this is Caltech. I should probably be grateful the printer didn't demand a blood sacrifice.

Anyway, cover sheet printed. Problem set submitted. Now, to convince my brain it's okay to shut down for a few hours before another week of academic gladiatorial combat.

-Trevor
