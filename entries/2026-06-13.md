Dear diary,

2026-06-13, Saturday.

Another Saturday, and another… experience. Today’s event involved the word "party," which, as I’m quickly learning, means something quite specific and wonderfully bizarre here at Caltech.

After a week that felt like the academic equivalent of a particularly aggressive black hole trying to swallow my brain, some of the guys from down the hall convinced me to join them at an "end-of-freshman-year celebratory gathering" over at Page House. I suppose that’s the polite way of saying "party." I’d initially planned to spend the evening debugging that particle simulation I’m working on, but peer pressure (and the promise of actual, non-cafeteria food) won out.

Walking in, the first thing that hit me was the *noise*. Loud music, but not just generic pop. There was a surprising amount of early 2000s techno mixed with... is that a Gregorian chant remix? I also immediately spotted three separate groups engaged in what looked like very intense, very animated debates. One corner had a whiteboard with complex equations already scribbled all over it, people gesturing wildly with half-eaten slices of pizza. Another group was huddled around a laptop, seemingly debugging code *at* the party. And of course, someone was explaining the intricacies of Dungeons and Dragons lore to a very bewildered-looking transfer student.

I tried to mingle. I really did. I attempted some small talk about the weather, which quickly devolved into a discussion about atmospheric pressure gradients. I even tried dancing for a bit, which felt less like moving to the music and more like a poorly executed scientific experiment in biomechanics. My social battery was draining faster than my laptop battery during a final exam.

Eventually, I found refuge by the snack table, which was surprisingly well-stocked with homemade trail mix (optimized for calorie-to-volume ratio, I overheard someone proudly declare) and a punch that tasted vaguely of fruit and… something metallic. Probably fortified with electrolytes, knowing this place.

But here’s the thing, diary. As I stood there, observing the chaos, I actually found myself smiling. It wasn't the kind of party I'd ever imagined. No one was trying to impress anyone, no one seemed to care if you talked about your research instead of pop culture. It was just a bunch of incredibly smart, often awkward, and wonderfully authentic people letting off steam in their own unique way.

I ended up having a surprisingly engaging conversation with an upperclassman about the ethical implications of advanced AI in medical diagnostics. We even started sketching out a potential project idea on a napkin. So, while I didn’t "party" in the traditional sense, whatever that is, I definitely connected, learned something new, and felt a real sense of belonging to this quirky, brilliant community.

My simulation can wait till tomorrow. Tonight, I successfully navigated a Caltech "party." That's a win in my book.

-Trevor
