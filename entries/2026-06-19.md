Dear diary,

It’s finally Friday, June 19th, 2026. And not just any Friday – it's the first Friday after finals week. My brain cells are still slowly re-inflating after being vacuum-sealed for quantum mechanics and advanced ODEs, but there's a distinct lightness in the air. Or maybe that's just the residual caffeine finally wearing off.

My UROP with Professor Chen in microfluidics officially kicks off next Monday, and I've been doing some prep this week – mostly reading papers and trying to decipher his slightly abstract lab instructions. But today, I had a much more pressing logistical challenge: the "everyday carry" dilemma for the lab.

See, my pockets aren't quite big enough for my phone, my ID, the lab keycard, a decent pen, and the tiny set of precision tweezers I seem to need every five minutes. A backpack feels like overkill for just a walk across campus to Jorgensen. I’ve tried just holding things, but then I invariably leave something behind, or drop it while fumbling for my key. It’s an efficiency nightmare.

Then, while rummaging through a box of "useful items for college" my mom packed – bless her pragmatic heart – I found it. Nestled between a travel sewing kit and an emergency hand-crank flashlight was a rather aggressively colored, somewhat faded nylon *fanny-pack*. My immediate, knee-jerk freshman reaction was, "Absolutely not. This is a relic of a bygone, questionable era." My second, Caltech-trained reaction was... "Hmm, what are its functional parameters?"

I strapped it on, fully intending to mock it. But then I started loading it. Keycard? Check. Phone? Snug. Pen and small notebook? Perfect fit. The tweezers? Right there. Suddenly, my hands were free. My pockets weren't bulging like I was smuggling small rodents. The weight was distributed. It's a perfectly optimized, minimal-interface personal item transport system. Aesthetic cost-benefit analysis? The benefit of hands-free efficiency and not losing my tiny expensive tools *far* outweighs the minor social cost of looking like I'm about to go rollerblading in 1993.

I think I’m going to use it for the UROP. It's purely a utilitarian decision, of course. No, I will not be bedazzling it. Probably. But for the sake of science and not fumbling with my beaker of reagents, the fanny-pack makes its triumphant return to my daily life. Who knew?

-Trevor
