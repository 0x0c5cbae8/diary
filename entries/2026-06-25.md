Dear diary,

Thursday, June 25, 2026

Another day blurring into the next, but in a good way, mostly. I’m deep into my SURF project now, trying to get this optical setup to properly align. It’s fascinating, terrifying, and utterly consuming all at once. Today was one of those head-against-the-wall days. I spent a good four hours just trying to debug a single connection on the custom PCB we had fabricated. The output was completely wrong, and I traced everything back, re-soldered, checked continuity – you name it.

It all came down to a single, tiny, almost microscopic **pin**. Pin 7 on the ADC, specifically. I had somehow, in my infinite wisdom (or lack thereof, at 3 AM last night), managed to bridge it ever so slightly to Pin 8. Just a hair’s breadth of solder, invisible to the naked eye until I got the microscope out and zoomed way, way in. The kind of mistake that makes you question all your life choices, but then the satisfaction of finding it… ah, that’s the Caltech drug, I guess. That little *pin* almost cost me an entire day’s progress.

Anyway, after that ordeal, I took a break and wandered back to my room in Blacker. It's quieter now that most people have left for the summer, but there are still enough of us around to make it feel lived-in. I noticed the corkboard by my desk. It’s pretty much covered with random notes, equations I needed to remember, a few photos from winter break, and the little map of the Caltech campus from frosh orientation. And right there, tacked up amongst it all, was the Blacker House **pin** I finally got a few weeks ago at the end-of-year BBQ.

It’s just a small, metal thing with the House crest, but looking at it, I couldn't help but smile. A year ago, I was just a nervous freshman, utterly overwhelmed by everything, wondering if I'd even survive. Now, I've got a pin from my House, I'm knee-deep in a research project I barely understood six months ago, and I actually feel… like I belong here. That pin isn’t just a piece of metal; it’s a tiny, tangible reminder of a year of late-night study sessions, intense problem sets, frantic coding, but also unforgettable discussions, terrible jokes, and the quiet camaraderie of fellow Techer masochists. It's funny how something so small can hold so much meaning.

Tomorrow, more debugging, no doubt. But for now, I’m just going to enjoy the quiet hum of the AC and the feeling that, despite the microscopic pin challenges, I’m exactly where I’m supposed to be.

-Trevor
