She talked about "hidden hunger" – when people eat *enough* calories, but their diets lack essential vitamins and minerals, leading to impaired cognitive function, weakened immune systems, and chronic fatigue.

And it hit me. I'm probably experiencing a milder, Caltech-specific form of that. I'm definitely not *starving*, but my brain feels fuzzy, my energy levels are consistently in the red zone, and I've been getting those weird eye twitches again. I'm consuming enough calories to fuel my frantic P-set sessions, but I'm absolutely certain I'm not getting the actual *nutrients* my brain and body need to function optimally. It's ironic, isn't it? We're at one of the world's most academically rigorous institutions, expected to perform at peak intellectual capacity, yet our lifestyle often leads to a state of self-induced dietary neglect that actively *hinders* that very capacity. My own brand of malnutrition, served with a side of existential dread.

I really need to figure out how to squeeze in some actual produce and protein. Maybe I'll grab an apple from the dhall before the dinner rush ends. Or, you know, just drink more coffee. It's basically a vitamin, right?
