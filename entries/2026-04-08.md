Dear diary,

Another Wednesday is almost over, and I swear the day-night cycle speeds up here. Just finished debugging my EE 12 project – a microcontroller controlling a small robotic arm. Spent about three hours staring at the same line of code, convinced the universe was actively conspiring against me. Then, suddenly, it was like something *thawed* in my brain. A tiny, almost imperceptible shift, and I saw the logic error. It wasn't the interrupt handler; it was the timing loop initialization! The relief was immense, like finally letting go of a breath I didn't realize I'd been holding.

That feeling of a mental *thaw* has been pretty common lately. Not just with problem sets, but with everything. When I first got here, it felt like my brain was a giant block of ice, crammed with information but unable to move or connect. Everything was stiff, cold, overwhelming. Now, slowly but surely, things are starting to... *melt*. I’m starting to see patterns, make connections, and even anticipate what's coming next, academically and socially.

Speaking of social, I actually joined the ultimate frisbee club's practice tonight for the first time. I’ve been meaning to for weeks, but honestly, just the thought of walking into a new group felt like navigating a minefield. But tonight, I just went for it. And it was... fine. More than fine, actually. People were friendly, taught me the ropes, and didn't laugh (too much) when I air-balled a throw. I felt a different kind of *thaw* there – less like a frozen statue and more like a normal human being, moving and interacting. It's weird, realizing how much I'd been holding myself back.

Even the weather here seems to be contributing. It was surprisingly warm today for April, hitting the mid-70s. I had a huge iced tea while I was debugging, and I watched the ice cubes quickly *thaw* and dissolve into the amber liquid. It felt symbolic, somehow. The rigid, frozen structures giving way to something fluid and adaptable.

I guess that's what being a freshman at Caltech is all about: constant thawing. Thawing out old ways of thinking, thawing out my social shell, thawing out stubborn engineering problems. It's exhausting, but also incredibly exhilarating when you realize you're actually making progress. Maybe by the end of the year, I'll be fully defrosted.

-Trevor
