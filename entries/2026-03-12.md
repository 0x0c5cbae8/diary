Dear diary,

Another Thursday has bled into Thursday night. Labs felt like they stretched into eternity today, culminating in a particularly baffling session with the Mach-Zehnder interferometer that refused to cooperate. Then, the new Quantum Mechanics 2b PSet dropped this morning, and it’s a monster. Seriously, Professor Chen must think we have an extra 48 hours in the week. My brain felt utterly *satiated* after staring at Fourier transforms for hours, definitely not *hungry* for another 10 problems on perturbation theory.

I was just staring at the wall of my dorm room, trying to summon the will to even open the PSet PDF, when the word "aperitif" randomly popped into my head. I think I heard one of the upperclassmen talking about it last week, explaining it to a visitor – something you drink before a meal to stimulate your appetite. And I thought, *that’s exactly what I need*. My intellectual appetite is completely gone, replaced by a dull ache of impending doom.

So, instead of just slamming my head against the PSet, I decided to try my own Caltech-style "aperitif." First, a strategic trip to Chandler for the strongest, darkest roast coffee they had, brewed so thick it could stand a spoon in it. While it was cooling, I didn't open the PSet. Instead, I pulled out that dusty book of logic puzzles I got for Christmas – the one with the really obscure Japanese diagram puzzles. I picked a moderately challenging one, something completely unrelated to physics, just pure pattern recognition and deduction.

It actually worked, weirdly enough. The coffee kicked in just as I solved the puzzle. My brain felt a little less like mush and a little more like a finely tuned machine, ready to tackle the complexities of hydrogen atom perturbations. It was a small, satisfying intellectual warm-up before the main course of quantum despair. Like a little palate cleanser for my neurons. Maybe I'm onto something here. No more diving straight into the existential dread. I just need a mental *aperitif* to get the engines running.

Wish me luck for the main "meal." It's going to be a long night.

-Trevor
