Dear diary,

May 4th, 2026. Monday. And already a disaster.

I woke up, or rather, was jolted awake by my alarm at 7:00 AM, feeling like I’d just closed my eyes five minutes ago. Another all-nighter for that brutal EE 10 p-set. The plan was solid: wake up, do a quick sanity check on my final circuits, transfer them neatly to the submission sheet, and barely make it to the EE drop-off before my 9 AM lecture. Simple. Logical. The kind of plan that thrives only in a world without Caltech’s unique brand of chaos.

The chaos today? The absolute, utter **misplacement** of my specific notebook containing the crucial calculations for the last two problems. Not *any* notebook, mind you. The one with the green cover that I specifically use for EE, the one where I had painstakingly worked out the tricky integrals for the filter design, the one that proved my resistor values were correct.

I tore my room apart. My desk, which usually resembles a supernova of textbooks and empty coffee cups, became an even more intense black hole of despair. Under the bed, in the closet, even in the *fridge* (don't ask, sometimes things end up there when I'm running on fumes). Nothing. My heart was pounding not from caffeine, but from pure panic. These weren't just scratch notes; these were the only detailed derivations I had to back up my final answers. If I just submitted the answers without verification, I might as well have thrown darts at a resistor bin.

It makes me wonder, diary, how much of my life here is defined by misplacement. It’s not just physical objects. Sometimes, in a particularly difficult lecture, I feel a sense of intellectual misplacement, like my brain cells have wandered off to a simpler university, leaving me behind to stare blankly at the blackboard. Or a social misplacement, when everyone else in my house seems to understand a joke or a cultural reference that completely flies over my head. It’s a constant battle to feel like all the pieces of *me* are where they're supposed to be, let alone my damn green notebook.

The irony is, I spent so much time perfectly *placing* my thoughts and calculations onto those pages, only for the entire book to be utterly *misplaced*. I finally found it, buried under a pile of discarded snack wrappers and old p-sets in the common room kitchenette, next to a perpetually half-empty bag of chips. Someone must have picked it up by mistake or just… left it there. My initial relief was quickly replaced by a wave of pure exhaustion.

I managed to quickly check my work, scribbling furiously, and submit the p-set with minutes to spare. But the entire ordeal just amplified the feeling of constant precariousness here. One wrong move, one moment of fatigue, and something critical gets misplaced, throwing everything into disarray. I just want to finish this term without losing my mind, my notes, or my sense of self.

Time for more coffee. And to figure out a better organizational system. Maybe.

-Trevor
