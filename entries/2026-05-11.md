Dear diary,

Ugh, Monday again. My internal clock is still convinced it’s Sunday evening, probably because I only got about three hours of proper sleep after finally wrestling that Abstract Algebra P-set into submission around 2 AM. Now it’s just past noon, and the caffeine hasn't quite kicked in enough to erase the phantom integrals dancing behind my eyelids.

We had our weekly Physics 2a study group this morning. It’s supposed to be helpful, and usually it is. There’s a problem on the new P-set about EM waves in a lossy dielectric, and it's a real beast. Sarah, one of the sophomores who always seems to breeze through everything, was explaining her approach. She had this whole derivation laid out on the whiteboard, sketching vectors and fields like it was second nature.

I was following along, trying to map her logic onto my own understanding. And then, I saw it. A tiny, subtle error in her initial assumption about the boundary conditions at the interface. Nothing major, but enough to propagate through the rest of the problem and lead to a slightly off final result. My brain started buzzing with how I could politely *interject*. I had the exact phrase ready: "Hey Sarah, could we just quickly clarify the assumption about the tangential components of E-field right at the surface? I was thinking..."

But she was already three steps ahead, scribbling furiously, explaining how her solution would simplify under specific parameters. Everyone else in the group was nodding along, taking notes. I felt this intense pressure, like if I *interjected* now, I’d sound pedantic, or worse, just plain wrong. What if *I* was the one misunderstanding? That stupid imposter syndrome really knows how to *interject* itself into my thoughts at the worst possible times.

So, I didn't say anything. I just watched as she finished, and everyone started packing up, feeling a fleeting sense of accomplishment before diving into their next torture device (aka, P-set). Now, I'm sitting here, staring at my half-eaten sandwich from Chandler, knowing I have to go back and re-work that entire problem on my own, just to confirm my suspicion. And I *know* it's going to lead me to a different answer than what Sarah presented.

It’s frustrating. This whole "speaking up" thing is harder than solving any differential equation. I need to get better at just, well, *interjecting* when I have a valid point or a question, even if it feels a little awkward. Maybe next time.

Time to drown my P-set sorrows in more coffee. Wish me luck.
