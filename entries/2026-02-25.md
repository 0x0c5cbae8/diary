Wednesday. Another Wednesday. Feels like the days are just a blur of lectures, problem sets, and trying to remember which equation goes where in Griffiths' Electrodynamics. Spent most of the afternoon wrestling with P-set 5 for Ph 1b – the one on boundary conditions and multipole expansions. My brain feels like it’s been through a centrifuge.

After a quick, probably unhealthy, dinner from Chandler (really need to venture out more, but *when*?), I finally collapsed onto my desk chair. Usually, this is where I'd open a physical textbook for Ma 1c, but tonight, I’m giving my new Kobo an honest go. I finally caved and bought a dedicated **e-reader** last week. My laptop screen was giving me chronic headaches, and trying to read dense proofs on my phone is just masochistic.

Honestly, this e-ink display is a game changer. The eye strain is practically non-existent compared to a backlit screen. I've loaded up the PDFs for our differential equations text, the supplemental readings for CS 1, and even a scanned copy of that obscure paper Dr. Lee mentioned in lecture today. It's ridiculous how much lighter my backpack is now. No more hauling around two physics books, a math book, and a lab notebook like I'm training for a weightlifting competition. The search function is also incredibly useful for finding specific definitions or theorems without flipping through a thousand pages.

Part of me still misses the tactile feel of physical pages, the smell of an old book, and the satisfying thud of putting it down. And annotating? While the stylus works, it’s not quite the same as scribbling furiously in the margins with a good old mechanical pencil. But practicality has to win out, especially here. With the sheer volume of material we have to get through, anything that shaves off a few minutes or a few pounds of stress is a godsend.

Right now, I'm trying to wrap my head around Fourier series on page 247. The convenience of having everything on this one device, easily searchable and portable, is starting to outweigh my romantic notions about paper. I think this e-reader and I are going to become very good friends this term.

Just need to survive till Friday. Maybe I'll allow myself to read something for *fun* on it this weekend, if I ever finish this P-set.
