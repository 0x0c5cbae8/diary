Dear diary,

My brain feels like a well-used CPU, overheating and ready for a reset. Just submitted that beast of a P-set for CS 21 – algorithmic analysis always finds a way to warp my sense of time. One minute it’s 3 PM, the next the deadline is looming like a final boss. It’s Sunday night, and I barely saw the sun today, again. Typical Caltech freshman life, I guess.

But then, the evening took an unexpected turn. Barely had I hit ‘submit’ when my phone buzzed with a cryptic message from Rishi, an upperclassman I know from my house: “R&R in 15. Bring your A-game.” R&R isn't "Rest & Relaxation" around here, it's code for "Research & Ruses," which usually means some kind of *caper* is afoot.

And boy, was it a caper. Not a lighthearted skip or a playful dance, but a full-blown, meticulously planned escapade. The target? The brand-new, super-fancy interactive LED art installation they just put up at UCLA's North Campus. Apparently, it's supposed to react to ambient sound and movement, creating abstract light patterns. Rishi and the crew have decided it needs a little Caltech "enhancement."

The plan, dubbed "Project Brightside," is to remotely hijack its DMX control system. My part, as the resident freshman eager beaver, is to finish reverse-engineering the public API they accidentally exposed (don't ask how Rishi found it) to inject our own pre-programmed light sequences. We're not doing anything destructive, just… *persuading* the installation to display something a little less abstract and a little more… *beaver-y*. And perhaps play a certain short, distinctive melody at high volume. Repeatedly.

My hands are actually shaking a little, partly from the triple espresso I chugged to finish the P-set, and partly from the sheer audacity of it all. It’s a rush, a different kind of problem-solving. It’s like a real-world puzzle with an incredibly high stake of "getting caught." But the thought of seeing that smug Bruin Bear bathed in flashing orange and white, accompanied by our fight song, is an intoxicating vision.

We’re heading out in an hour. My backpack is already loaded with my laptop, a portable Wi-Fi antenna (just in case), and enough snacks to fuel an overnight coding marathon. This is what they mean when they talk about the "Caltech experience" beyond the textbooks, isn't it? A different kind of intellectual exercise, wrapped in mischief and adrenaline. Wish me luck. Or rather, wish Project Brightside success.

-Trevor
