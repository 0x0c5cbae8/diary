Dear diary,

Another Sunday. Woke up a bit later than I intended, but honestly, after debugging that physics problem set until 2 AM, it was a necessity. The sun’s pretty bright through my window, even if it does make me squint when I finally get out of bed. It’s hard to believe it’s mid-March already; feels like just yesterday I was trying to figure out which end of the wrench went where during Rotation.

This morning, as I walked down the hall to the common room for some cold brew (bless the upperclassmen who maintain that machine), I caught myself thinking about the word "residence." It's funny, before I got here, I just thought of it as "dorm." A room, a bed, a desk. Functional. But now, six months in, it's so much more. This isn't just a building where I sleep; it’s… my house. My home, even.

When I first arrived, everything felt so chaotic and new. I remember standing in my assigned room, a twin bed, a desk, a dresser, and feeling a little lost. The walls seemed so bare, and the hallway full of strangers. Now, the walls are covered in a mix of P-set memes, whiteboard scribbles from late-night brainstorming, and a slightly-too-large poster of a black hole that my roommate Leo insisted on. The hallway isn’t full of strangers; it’s full of friendly (if sometimes sleep-deprived) faces.

Just last night, after I finally submitted that P-set, I ended up in the common room, and there were a bunch of us – freshmen and a couple of juniors – just talking, not about physics or math, but about random stuff, debating the merits of various sci-fi shows, planning a late-night run to In-N-Out. It’s that constant, low hum of activity, the open doors, the shared frustrations, and the unexpected camaraderie that makes this place more than just a living space. It’s a support system. It’s where I learned what froshing actually entails (and how to avoid some of the messier parts). It's where I found my first real Caltech friends, the ones who understand why I'd spend five hours on a single problem and then immediately want to talk about it for another hour.

I guess what I’m trying to say is that this "residence" has utterly transformed my Caltech experience. It’s not just a place to study and sleep, it's the heart of my social life, my academic support network, and honestly, a huge part of my identity here. Knowing I'll be in this house for the next three years is incredibly comforting. It makes the insane workload feel manageable, knowing there’s always someone down the hall who gets it, or at least has a spare cup of ramen.

Time to tackle that chemistry reading before dinner. Probably another impromptu study group will form in the lounge later. That’s just how it is here.

-Trevor
