Dear diary,

Another Thursday down, and my brain feels like it’s been through a centrifuge. Quantum Mechanics problem set 8 is officially due tomorrow, and I swear I can *feel* the Hilbert space expanding in my head. Then there was that lab report for ChE 105 – calculating the mass transfer coefficient for a packed bed column, which, let me tell you, is way less exciting than it sounds, especially when your data points are scattered like a cat’s breakfast.

But all that pales in comparison to the existential dread brought on by… *the resume*. Yeah, you heard me. R-E-S-U-M-E. Today, after my last class, I wandered into that "SURF Info Session for Freshmen" in the Bechtel Auditorium, mostly for the free pizza (classic Caltech move, I know). And all they talked about was how we should already be polishing our resumes for summer research opportunities. *Polishing*? Diary, my resume is currently a crumpled piece of paper that vaguely lists my high school robotics club and the time I debugged my grandma’s Wi-Fi. It’s barely a *rough draft* of a resume, let alone something ready for a Caltech professor’s discerning eye.

The career services lady kept saying, "Highlight your skills! Showcase your projects! Quantify your achievements!" I started mentally scrolling through my life, trying to find anything that wasn't "successfully survived organic chemistry" or "can function on three hours of sleep." My "skills" feel like they belong on a LinkedIn profile for a very ambitious middle schooler: proficient in Python (for basic scripting), excellent at procrastinating until the last minute, can identify at least three different types of instant coffee.

Honestly, it's a bit disheartening. Everyone here is brilliant, and I just assume they all arrived with fully fleshed-out resumes, complete with published papers and patents. I mean, what do I even *put* on a resume right now that screams "future Nobel laureate" instead of "has a strong interest in ramen"? I feel like I need to *do* something significant just to *have* something significant to write down. It’s a bit of a chicken-and-egg problem. How do I get a research position to build my resume if my resume isn’t impressive enough to get a research position? My brain started trying to solve *that* paradox instead of the QM problems.

Anyway, I guess I'll spend some time this weekend staring at that blank Word document. Maybe I can frame "successfully built a stable trebuchet in high school physics" as "demonstrated proficiency in mechanical design and projectile motion physics, achieving optimal trajectory simulation." Or perhaps "maintained above-average GPA in Caltech's notoriously rigorous first-year curriculum" is a good start. It feels like I'm trying to stretch a single sheet of paper to cover an entire bed.

It's just another reminder of how much there is to do, and how quickly time flies. Gotta go; that quantum mechanics won't solve itself, and I need *something* for my future resume, even if it's just "successfully completed QM 8."

-Trevor
